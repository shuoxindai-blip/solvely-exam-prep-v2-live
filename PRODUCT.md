# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Students using Solvely.ai on mobile while studying, preparing for exams, or turning class material into guided practice. They are usually in a focused study session and need fast capture, clear interpretation, and trustworthy next steps.

## Product Purpose

Solvely.ai helps students move from raw learning material or a homework problem into structured understanding: scan or enter content, confirm what the system understood, review step-by-step reasoning, create study artifacts, and continue practicing. Success means the interface makes the next study action obvious without hiding the student's input, plan, or reasoning trail.

## Brand Personality

Calm, supportive, and precise. The product should feel like a native study workspace: approachable enough for repeated homework use, disciplined enough to trust before an exam, and fast enough to keep students in flow.

## Anti-references

Do not make Solvely.ai feel like a generic AI marketing site, a purple-gradient chatbot, a decorative productivity dashboard, or a theme-preview playground. Avoid fake logos, fake performance claims, buried answers, weak placeholder copy, and inactive controls that look tappable.

## Design Principles

- Keep the study task visible: input, interpretation, plan, answer, steps, and next action should be easy to locate.
- Make one primary action obvious at each moment; secondary choices should support the current phase instead of competing with it.
- Use native-feeling mobile patterns, compact spacing, generous touch targets, and clear state feedback.
- Build trust through transparent progress, recoverable errors, and concrete study language.
- Let color serve action and status: blue for learning context and selection, green for confirmation or solve progress.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls, maintain 44px minimum touch targets, avoid color-only status communication, and respect reduced-motion preferences. Exam-prep flows should work with short and long topic names, typed or voice input, empty states, and recoverable invalid input.
